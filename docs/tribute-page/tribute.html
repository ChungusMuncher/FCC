Stefan Karl Stefansson

He was number one.

Stefan Karl Stefansson was a beacon of positivity and hope to all.

Some info on Stefan:

10th July 1975: Born in Hafnarfjörður, Iceland.

1991: Begins to spend a lot of time at a theatre in his home town, kickstarting his acting career.

1994: Begins work as a puppeteer for television.

1999: Finally graduates from the Drama Academy of Iceland. He then travelled the country, performing a show over 170 times.

2002: Established the Regnbogabörn (Rainbow Children) campaign to assist bullying and assist bullying victims. Stefan spent hours sat down with children who were at risk of suicide, to talk to them about their problems and assist them.

2003-2007, 2013-2014: Approached by Magnús Scheving, an Icelandic Gymnast who was dissatisfied with the lack of healthy lifestyle role-models for children. He created the show Lazy Town, starring himself as the protagonist Sportacus, a healthy athlete who inspires the residents of Lazy Town to get fit and healthy, and Robbie Rotten, a man who wanted to stay indoors and sleep, played by Stefan. The show was picked up by Nickelodeon, and televised internationally for many years, inspiring children around the world to lead a more active lifestyle.

October 2016: Diagnosed with bile duct cancer. Around this time, a song from Lazy Town titled 'We Are Number One' became an internet meme, greatly increasing his notoriety. He did an AMA on Reddit, where he responsed with warmth and positivity to all comments and well-wishes from fans.

March 2018: Cancer progresses to an inoperable stage. Begins chemotherapy to prolong life.

April 2018: Decided to discontinue chemotherapy, and shut down all of his social media accounts to cut out all negativity (in the form of the news and mass media) from his life.

June 2018: Awarded the Order of the Falcon, the highest honour for a citizen of Iceland, for his contribution to Icelandic acting, as well as Icelandic society for his work with Rainbow Children.

21st August 2018: Passes away from cancer.

26th September 2018: His fans band together to award him the Youtube Golden Play Button posthumously.


"It’s not until they tell you you’re going to die soon that you realize how short life is. Time is the most valuable thing in life because it never comes back. And whether you spend it in the arms of a loved one or alone in a prison-cell, life is what you make of it. Dream big." ~Stefan Karl Stefansson
